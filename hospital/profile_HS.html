About:

Act 36 established the 200-bed National Hospital for Women and Children in 1999. Presidentially. Its main goals were to help Nigerian and West African women and children, reduce morbidity and mortality, and investigate their particular diseases. National Hospital was renamed and obliged to serve everyone in 2000. The Federal Ministry of Health took over administration from the Presidency in 2011. After adding 400 beds, the hospital now offers all medical specializations.
